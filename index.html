How the XD practice built an AI-powered opportunity engine on Gong, EVO, and Claude — and stopped losing pipeline before the conversation started.

Caylent XD

The Problem

"The XD team had capacity.
What it didn't have was visibility."

Deals were scoped and sold without design. By the time XD heard about an opportunity, the SOW was signed, the team was staffed, and the door was already closed.

The Transformation

From Manual Hunt to AI-Powered Pipeline

Before
  • —Manually hunting EVO, reading SOWs and proposals one by one
  • —Watching Gong calls + using internal Gong AI summaries
  • —Checking opp Slack channels manually, just asking people
  • —Manually inserting each find into the VRU tracking sheet
  • —Everyone using a different prompt — inconsistent criteria, stale and duplicate opps
→
Now
  • ✓Claude scans Slack, EVO, Drive, and Gong transcripts via MCP connectors
  • ✓Cross-checks VRU sheet automatically — no duplicates reach the board
  • ✓Same system prompt across the entire XD team — one consistent standard
  • ✓Signals ranked by relevance — XD reviews and decides, doesn't re-hunt
  • ✓Claude proposes all updates — nothing writes without human sign-off
The System

Meet the Void Reclamation Unit

Go where no one is looking. Dig through the mess. Find the value others missed.

The Mission

Hunt missed, overlooked, or under-scoped design opportunities. Ensure XD has a seat at the table before scope is locked and teams are staffed — not after.

Signal Detection

Four signal types: language ("we'll figure out UX later"), project scope (AI, dashboards, portals), risk gaps (no MVP, no adoption plan), team behavior (no designer staffed).

Crew Model

Scavenger finds it. Hunter qualifies it. Interceptor gets XD in the room. Reclaimer converts it. Recovery Lead (Mel) owns strategy and prioritization.

The AI Workflow

How Claude Does the Hunting

/vru-scan

Scan for signals

Scans Slack, EVO, Google Drive, and Gong transcripts using the VRU signal library. Returns a ranked list of opportunities with a recommended next step for each. Runs on demand or triggered manually by the team.

/vru-update

Sync the board

Reads every active opportunity, pulls recent Slack and EVO activity per client, flags overdue next actions, and proposes updates. Nothing writes to the board without explicit approval.

/vru-log

Log a new opportunity

Walks through qualification using the signal framework, auto-assigns priority and next action, generates the next Opp ID, and previews everything before committing to the board.

Human in the Loop

Claude Surfaces. XD Decides.

The AI handles volume and consistency. The XD team brings context, judgment, and expertise that no model can replicate.

What Claude does

  • —Scans hundreds of data points across every connected source simultaneously
  • —Applies the signal library consistently — same criteria, every time, no fatigue
  • —Surfaces ranked signals and proposes next steps for review
  • —Never writes to the board or sends messages without explicit sign-off

What XD brings

  • ✓Client context Claude can't see — relationships, history, team dynamics
  • ✓Judgment on which signals are real vs. noise based on domain expertise
  • ✓Bandwidth decisions — what to pursue, what to park, what to let go
  • ✓The intercept itself — no AI replaces the AE relationship and the UX pitch

Every item on the VRU board has been reviewed and approved by a member of the XD team. Claude accelerates discovery. Judgment belongs to us.

The Stack

Built on MCP Connectors + Claude Cowork

The entire system runs inside Claude's Cowork environment. Each data source is a live MCP connector — Claude queries them directly in the conversation, no copy-paste, no tab-switching, no manual exports.

One known constraint: Gong cannot be connected directly yet. Transcripts are accessed via EVO and Slack — still high-signal, but a native Gong MCP would meaningfully expand coverage.

Honest Reflection

What We've Learned

What works

Speed of discovery

Claude surfaces signals across all sources in minutes — what used to eat hours of individual hunting

One standard across XD

Shared system prompt means every find is measured against the same signal library — no more personal judgment gaps

Cleaner board, less noise

Deduplication runs automatically — stale opps and already-staffed deals no longer clog the pipeline

Still working on it

No direct Gong connection

Transcripts via EVO and Slack are useful but incomplete — a native Gong MCP would be a significant unlock

Adoption takes reinforcement

The board only works if the team logs consistently — building the habit took longer than building the system

Signal tuning is ongoing

Early scans were noisy — the signal library improves with every hunt and is a living document

Wrap-Up

The Short Version

TLDR

We replaced hours of fragmented manual hunting with a Claude-powered workflow that scans our pipeline signals, deduplicates against our tracker, and surfaces ranked opportunities — so XD can focus on the intercept, not the discovery.

Key lessons

01

Build the signal library first. The AI is only as good as the criteria you give it.

02

Standardization is the unlock. One shared prompt eliminated inconsistency overnight.

03

The hard part is behavior change. Building the system took days. Logging consistently took much longer.

How it works in practice

Claude scans on demand

Runs /vru-scan across all connected sources. The daily Slack digest is triggered separately via a Google Apps Script connected to the VRU Board.

XD reviews the output

We qualify signals using domain expertise, cross-check the board, decide what to pursue.

Log it and intercept

/vru-log captures the opp. The Interceptor reaches out to the AE directly in the opp Slack channel.

Board stays current

/vru-update flags overdue actions weekly. Team approves changes in one pass.
